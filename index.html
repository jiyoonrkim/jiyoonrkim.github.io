About

Hello! My name is Jiyoon Kim, and I'm a Ph.D. student in Communication Sciences and Disorders at Northwestern University. I am a member of the Language, Education, and Reading Neuroscience (LEARN) Lab, advised by Dr. Elizabeth Norton.

I completed a M.A. in Non-Clinical Communication Sciences at Northwestern University, and I completed B.A.s in Linguistics and Psychology at Seoul National University in Seoul, South Korea.

My research uses behavioral and computational methods to study reading development, reading difficulties, and the cognitive processes underlying reading in the brain. I am especially interested in identifying early markers of dyslexia and improving the assessment of reading ability, with the goal of developing more accurate and efficient tools to predict future reading outcomes. [Link to CV]

Research Interests

  • Early cognitive and behavioral predictors of reading difficulties in children
  • Psychometric assessment and measurement of language and reading skills
  • Reading development and reading-related cognitive processes
